The Morpho genus ?

Morpho butterflies are one of the poster childs of the Lepidoptera genus ! As they are quite large and arbor a bright iridescent blue (in most species), they are quite popular among collectors and amateurs alike. But funnily enough, all this public attention does not translate into the research world quite as well. Here is a spinning Morpho !

Why are they so blue ?

As you may have noticed, the shade of blue reflected in Morpho wings changes depending on the angle you look at them ! This is due to the color not being a physical (pigment) color, but a structural color. This means that the structure and arrangement of the scales themselves that make it so only blue light is reflected. Think of it like a some kind of weird reverse prism, where instead of turning white light into a rainbow, it turns it into a single color. Add Morpho + zoom on scales here
